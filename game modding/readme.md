## It all started with a simple question:

**"What if I played games differently?"**

Even after finishing a game, I always enjoyed finding new ways to experience it.

For example, I would challenge myself to complete a game by resolving every possible conflict through dialogue instead of combat.

That curiosity led me to experiment with games in all kinds of unconventional ways.

> I simply enjoyed asking these kinds of **"what if?"** questions and seeing where they led.

That curiosity naturally evolved into an interest in modding.

Have you ever had one of those strange but surprisingly entertaining ideas?

Like,

*"What if I just lived comfortably as a zombie during a zombie apocalypse?"*

**Project Zomboid** — Using debug mode and Lua scripting, I created a playthrough where I survived as a zombie.

Over time, that mindset became the foundation of how I approach development.


**I build systems by breaking them, understanding them, and optimizing them under real constraints.**

Today, I focus on projects that are not only enjoyable to build but also practically useful, applying the same way of thinking beyond games to real-world software and engineering.

**Analyzing systems by breaking and rebuilding them.**
